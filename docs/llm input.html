4. Projects & Case Studies

Purpose: Show how you think and what you actually did, with outcomes.

Structure: Each case study with the same template:

  • Context
  • Challenge
  • Actions
  • Results / Impact
  • Tools & Methods

Suggested case studies from your background:

  1. Case Study 1 – Digital Workplace Optimization for IT Department

    • Context: KPMG US IT department, 2,500 employees, 9,000+ users of collaboration platforms.
    • Challenge: Fragmented tools, inactive accounts, unclear governance.
    • Actions: Redesigned governance framework; cleaned up accounts; drove adoption of collaboration tools.
    • Results: Reduced inactive accounts by ~5,000 and generated significant annual savings.
    • Tools: MS Teams, SharePoint, Confluence, ServiceNow.
    • Theme: Knowledge management, digital workplace transformation, cost optimization.

  2. Case Study 2 – AI & GenAI Adoption Program

    • Context: Same KPMG environment.
    • Challenge: Make AI tools understandable and useful for non-technical staff.
    • Actions: Designed newsletters, internal webinars, training materials, and a hackathon focused on AI use cases.
    • Results: Increased awareness and use of AI tools, new ideas for process automation.
    • Theme: AI adoption strategy, change management.

  3. Case Study 3 – Productivity Mentoring Program (Forte Labs Course)

    • Context: 30+ professionals across APAC participating in a productivity course.
    • Challenge: Help busy professionals improve time management and knowledge workflows.
    • Actions: 1:1 mentoring, practical workflows, digital organization systems.
    • Results: 85% reported measurable improvements in productivity and knowledge workflows.
    • Theme: Coaching, productivity systems, behavior change.

  4. Case Study 4 – ERP Implementation for SMEs

    • Context: Terra Colligo Latam consulting engagements.
    • Challenge: SMEs lacking integrated systems for finance, inventory, and operations.
    • Actions: Process mapping, requirement gathering, Odoo ERP configuration, training.
    • Results: Improved reporting, reduced manual work, better visibility into operations.
    • Theme: Digital transformation, business process redesign.

  5. Case Study 5 – AI in Education Research Project

    • Context: Research at University Canada West on AI-generated feedback and student perceptions.
    • Challenge: Understand how students perceive AI-graded feedback and fairness.
    • Actions: Co-designing study, NVivo coding of interviews, drafting and submitting academic paper.
    • Results: Manuscript submitted to a peer-reviewed journal; findings on student trust and fairness perceptions.
    • Theme: Research design, qualitative methods, AI in education.
